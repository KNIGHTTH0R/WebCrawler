Election Watch

Coalition consensus must
be reached tonight, PPP
regional branches say

The Jakarta Post, Jakarta | Election Watch | Sun, May 11 2014, 8:07 PM
Election Watch News
  • Golkar lawmaker shrugs off VP issue
  • PKS officially backs Gerindra
  • Hanura joins PDI-P camp

Current tough lobbying processes on the formation of a coalition for the presidential race have drawn complaints from the United Development Party’s (PPP) regional executive board members who are attending the party’s second national leaders meeting (Rapimnas) in Jakarta on Sunday.

The party’s South Sulawesi executive board chairman, Amir Uskara, said lobbying conducted internally had been less productive because the concept being offered on whether PPP should form a coalition with the Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle (PDI-P) or the Gerindra Party was not clear enough.

He said the party’s regional branches demanded that the PPP coalition plans must be decided in the following meeting on Sunday evening.

“We have been in discussion over the coalition for some time now, right? So, we ask all parties involved in the Rapimnas to make sure that the coalition-related matters are resolved this evening,” he said on Sunday as quoted by kompas.com.

Amir said party leaders involved in the process had been divided into two groups. The first group supported a coalition with the Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle (PDI-P), which nominated Joko “Jokowi” Widodo as its presidential candidate, while the other preferred a coalition with the Gerindra Party, with Prabowo Subianto as presidential candidate.

Meanwhile, PPP secretary general M.Romahurmuzy’s suggestion on a possible coalition between PPP and the Golkar Party was not well received by most of the party’s regional executive board officials.

With such differing views, it was said the PPP’s coalition plans for the presidential race remained unclear.

“We, the officials of the PPP regional executive boards, want the party’s syuro [consultative] board to have a clear direction,” said Amir.

“Please don’t just say that we have to form a coalition with Jokowi because he is a good person, unlike Prabowo [...], and vice versa. Such a view is very subjective,” he went on.

Amir further said: “What we want to know is, if we form a coalition with Jokowi or Prabowo, what kind of national agenda PPP will put forward and whether such a concept is compatible with the party or how it will affect the interests of Muslim communities in Indonesia, because PPP is a big house for Islamic parties in the country.”

Another issue that has to be clarified is with regards to PPP’s position in the government if it decides to form a coalition either with PDI-P or Gerindra.

“Will we be offered a vice presidential position or is there something else? We want a clearer picture so we can move forward in our discussion,” said Amir.

Separately, Romahurmuzy confirmed that the second Rapimnas would commence at 7 p.m., on Sunday. He refused to provide details on whether PPP would have decided the direction of the party coalition in the meeting this evening. (gda/ebf)
