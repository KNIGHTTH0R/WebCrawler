Ageas Reports 30% Growth in Asia's Business

World | May 15, 2014

HONG KONG, May 15, 2014 /PRNewswire/ — Ageas Announces 3 month results 2014 (Note 1) — Asia Financial Highlights
Ageas reports growing inflows, UK storms & floods impact insurance profit
— Net profit of insurance operations was EUR 145 million
— Group inflows (at 100%) at EUR 7.8 billion (+15%), largely driven by growth in Life inflows in Asia (+32%)
— Group net profit of EUR 30 million
— Insurance solvency ratio at 209%; Group solvency ratio at 213%. General Account net cash position of EUR 1.8 billion (vs. EUR 1.9 billion at the end of 2013)
Strong Life new business growth while maintaining good underlying profit in Asia
— Ageas Asia’s net profit at EUR 39 million vs. EUR 37 million (+3%) of which EUR 7 million originated from its Hong Kong operations
— Asia’s inflows at EUR 4.4 billion vs. EUR 3.4 billion (+30%)
o Mainland China’s inflows increased 42% to EUR 3.4 billion, with new business premiums up 59% to EUR 2.3 billion. The bank channel and the agency channel both contributed to this growth.
o Thailand’s Life inflows were up 18% to EUR 446 million. Life new business premiums were up 28% to EUR 212 million. Non-Life inflows were up 5% to EUR 57 million across all lines of business.
o Malaysia’s life inflows amounted to EUR 123 million. Non-Life premiums were EUR 178 million.
o Hong Kong’s inflows increased 5% to EUR 110 million. New business premiums increased by 6%.
o India’s inflows were EUR 36 million
— Strong solvency in Asia (including non-consolidated operations) at 228%
Announcing the 3 month results 2014, Gary Crist, Chief Executive Officer of Ageas Asia commented:
“Asia maintained the good momentum from 2013 into the first quarter. Life inflows were up 32% with higher sales driven by Mainland China and Thailand. Life new business premiums grew in both the bank and agency channels as they benefited from successful sales campaigns and continued channel development, including a strong increase in the number of agents. Renewal premiums were again up significantly as a result of last year’s strong sales and continued good persistency across all entities.”
Please visit http://www.ageas.com for full details of the press release.
Note:
1. All 3 month 2014 data are compared to the 3 month 2013 figures unless otherwise stated.
