‘Million Dollar Listing’
holds out for Asia deals

Tertiani ZB Simanjuntak, The Jakarta Post, Jakarta | Entertainment | Thu, May 08 2014, 1:21 PM
Reality: The series shows the aggressive, high-stakes world of New York City’s real estate market. (Courtesy of Life Inspired)

In New York, a battle between three men set among the backstreets and alleyways of the Big Apple, over millions of dollars is set to take place.

The highly popular reality TV series Million Dollar Listing New York, currently in its third season, is now available for viewing in Asia.

The documentary series follows three real estate agents, giving viewers an inside look into the aggressive, high-stakes world of New York City’s real estate market as they close multiple deals within just a few days.

The series is a spin-off of Million Dollar Listing Los Angeles, which premiered on March 2012.

Produced by the World Wonder for Bravo TV, the first season reportedly averaged 1.04 million viewers in the US.

The first season introduced brokers Fredrik Eklund, Ryan Serhant and Michael Lorber; each of whom have unique tricks and traits in getting clients, handling the fierce competition in the property market and, of course, among each other.

Dogfights and dirty tricks are expected in each of the nine episodes as the aggressive trio of brokers put their best foot forward in the race, although it means they have to contend with run-ins with each other.

Starting Thursday, the program will air on Life Inspired channel for viewers in Indonesia, Malaysia, Brunei Darussalam, Singapore, Thailand, Hong Kong and Taiwan. The one-hour unscripted program starts at 8 p.m. Jakarta time.  

The paid television channel is available through various media platforms, including First Media, Indovision, Max3, Big TV, K Vision, Telkomvision, Aora, Topas TV and Viva Sky.

Life Inspired general manager Anne Chan said that the series was among the channel’s key shows this year.

“The US$3 million hunks in this hit series are not only extremely successful in what they do, we believe their charming personalities and engaging TV presence will definitely appeal to our target audience,” she said.

There has been criticism on the actual reality captured on the show.

In his defense, Eklund said that the series was not controlled by producers.

“Filming a reality show is like jumping out of an airplane,” he said as quoted by The New York Times. “You just hope at some point on the way down that parachute is going to go off. I am a control freak in real estate. But you cannot control this, there is no way.”

Meet the three million-dollar men

Ryan Serhant: (Courtesy of Life Inspired)

The Player
Ryan Serhant

The former hand model and soap actor is not shy when describing his best traits as a broker. “[They are] my good looks and charm,” he said in an email interview.

Serhant, a broker with Nest Seekers International, was picked for the show as he had sold nearly
$1 billion in real estate in less than five years, making him one of the youngest, most successful agents.

He said his business “has grown 10,000 percent and I did not expect that to happen” as the result of joining in the show.

He had moved from an office above a burger joint to his own space in the elite TriBeCa, Lower Manhattan, and went from having one assistant to a team of 12, with perks including a luxury SUV with a driver.

While real estate is his primary focus, he continues to develop as an actor, writer and producer. In 2013, he created, produced, and starred in his own web-series, Ryan’s Realty Byte for Celebuzz.com, and just wrapped work on his first feature film, Noah Baumbach’s While We’re Young with Ben Stiller, Naomi Watts and Amanda Seyfried.

His achievements have been recognized by leading trade organizations, which have invited him to deliver keynote speeches, including Lehigh Valley Association of
Realtors, The ACS Home Show, Real Estate Connect 2014, the annual convention for the world’s largest real estate association and The National Association of Realtors.

During his spare time, Serhant is a budding philanthropist who supports Habitat for Humanity, Make a Wish Foundation, Share Our Strength and Charity Water.

Seeing his life as a process of constant change and evolution, Serhant described himself as an educated, thoughtful, aggressive, ethical and fair kind of a broker — although his competitors might not agree with him.

His best advice for struggling real estate agents: “My dad taught me to always be the first one in the office and the last one to leave. You will only get out of life what you put into it.” And that’s a good deal.

Hot tips:

1. Real estate is a Darwinian struggle for survival.
2. Web listings are often false.
3. Listings are littered with misleading sales speak.
4. If you can’t see it, agents don’t mention it.
5. Open houses are more for the agent than the seller or buyer.
6. Agents “double end” you to get bigger commissions.
7. You don’t need to hire a pricey company to help stage your home.

Michael Lorber: (Courtesy of Life Inspired)

The Rookie
Michael Lorber

Lorber is Director of Business Development for Douglas Elliman Worldwide Consulting. Since joining the company in 2006, he is a top producer for the firm focusing on International Project Marketing and multimillion dollar properties throughout Manhattan.

Being the son of New York real estate giant Howard Lorber, his rise in the real estate sector was a natural progression with his passion starting at an early age when he would attend open houses with his father.

A native of Long Island’s South Shore, Lorber graduated from the business school at Babson College with a BS in Real Estate and Finance.

He spent summers working for Donald Trump while in college before attending law school at Benjamin N. Cardozo School of Law specializing in tax law. After passing the New York State Bar Exam, Michael earned his MS in Real Estate at New York University.

As an attorney, Lorber got his brokerage license to pursue a passion for real estate.

Hot tips:

1. Buy with your head (and wallet), not your heart.
2. Consider the motivation: lifestyle or investment.
3. Always do your research! (The Internet can be your best friend or worst enemy.)
4. You can’t afford not to use a realtor. (We work for you!)
5. Know what you don’t know.
6. Short-term renting may be best.
7. Consider the cost of money.
8. Be aware of the big picture (monthly fees, maintenance, taxes, etc.) so you’re not out-of-pocket
every month.
9. If it sounds too good to be true, it usually is.
10. Location, location, location.

Fredrik Eklund: (Courtesy of Life Inspired)

The Shark
Fredrik Eklund

Eklund, the energetic, high-kicking, top broker at Douglas Elliman has secured over two billion dollars in closed residential sales. Constantly rated as New York City’s “Top Luxury Broker” and was nominated as “Rookie of the Year” by the Real Estate Board of New York, he set record sales in 28 buildings in Manhattan last year alone and is often spotted showing apartments to top name celebrities. His specialty is New Development and currently is working on launching 15 new buildings in the next year.

Originally from Stockholm, Sweden, Eklund studied at the Stockholm School of Economics before starting an internet company and working for investment bank SEB in Stockholm, London, Singapore and Tokyo. He is also the founder of Scandinavia’s most high-end residential real estate brokerage with 50 employees and $600 million in closed sales in 2013. Eklund founded the best top-producing team at Elliman, which has 5,000 agents in 85 offices all over the East Coast.

He is known for being part “shark” in business and part “softie” at home with his puppies and family.

Thanks to the show, Eklund saw his team’s gross commissions nearly double, to $5.27 million, and last year he and his partner, John Gomes, and 10 others were the second-ranked broker team within the brokerage, having risen from eighth place the year before.

Hot tips:

1. Listing photos have to be perfect.
2. Take out all the clutter and items that are too personal.
3. Clean thoroughly and repaint the entire space.
4. Pick a broker with a broad outreach.
5. Being a broker is about energy: If your agent doesn’t have the right energy, the property will not sell.
