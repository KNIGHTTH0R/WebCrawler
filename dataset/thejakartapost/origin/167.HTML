Entries Now Open for British Business Awards 2014 after Successful Launch

Business | May 16, 2014

BEIJING, May 16, 2014 /PRNewswire/ — The official launch event of the British Business Awards 2014 (BBA) was held recently at the British Ambassador’s residence in Beijing. Sebastian Wood, HM Ambassador to China, delivered the opening remarks at the event, which was attended by some 150 guests, including members of both BritCham and CBBC. The launch marked the opening of entries for the 2014 Awards, which applicants can now submit via the official BBA website at http://www.britishbusinessawards.cn until 27 August.
(Photo: http://photos.prnasia.com/prnh/20140516/0861403326-a )
The launch event was held in conjunction with the BritCham/CBBC British Business Briefing, at which sector experts from the UK Embassy deliver free monthly updates to BritCham/CBBC members on the political and economic landscape. Sebastian Wood, Patron of the British Business Awards 2014, delivered welcoming remarks at the launch in which he urged British and Chinese companies and individuals to participate in this year’s Awards and to demonstrate their achievements in UK-China business, a message which was underlined by Ralph Rogers, Director of the British Chamber of Commerce in China and the China-Britain Business Council, North China.
(Photo: http://photos.prnasia.com/prnh/20140516/0861403326-b )
“As the British Ambassador to China, I’m delighted by the continuing achievements of the British Business Awards. The UK Government’s confidence is underlined by its support of the organisation’s far-ranging expansion throughout China in 2014. This is a strengthening of partnership between Britain and China and will substantially increase the support for British companies working with China,” said Sebastian Wood, Her Majesty’s Ambassador and Patron of the British Business Awards 2014.
“I am excited about the launch of our fifth British Business Awards in China. There are many success stories of British companies doing business in and with China, of British-educated alumni and of Chinese firms doing business in and with the UK. The BBA is a great platform to showcase these success stories and we urge people to come forward and share these, to be participants and to be involved,” said Russell Brown, Chairman of the British Chamber of Commerce in China and Vice-Chair of the China-Britain Business Council.
For each award category there will be a panel of five judges, including one lead judge, composed of prominent members of British and Chinese industry and government. Entries close on 27 August, and online voting will be open between 10 and 27 September. The judging panels will then review the applications and shortlist five candidates per award. The judges will interview representatives of the five finalists in each category and submit recommendations to the advisory panel for their approval. The eight winners will be revealed at the British Business Awards 2014 Gala Dinner, to be held on 20 November in Beijing.

About the British Business Awards
The British Business Awards( http://www.britishbusinessawards.cn ) are held every two years and have grown in stature to become a prestigious feature of the business calendar since 2008. The 2014 Awards, to be held in Beijing on 20 November, are endorsed by the British Ambassador, Sebastian Wood, and the Gala Dinner awards ceremony regularly attracts close to 400 eminent guests from Chinese and British business. Consisting of eight categories, the Awards highlight organisations and individuals from a variety of disciplines who are achieving outstanding results and who demonstrate commitment to trade and investment in China. They also recognise the accomplishments of Chinese businesses operating in the UK. They are organised by the British Chamber of Commerce in China and the China-Britain Business Council and supported by the British Chambers of Commerce in the UK, the independent British Chambers of Commerce Guangdong and Shanghai, British Council, the Confederation of British Industry, UK Trade & Investment, Walpole British Luxury and the 48 Group Club. The 2014 BBA Awards are sponsored by British Council, BP China, ICAEW, Jaguar Land Rover Automotive Trading (Shanghai) Co., Ltd., Locate Jersey and Manchester Business School of the University of Manchester and UKTI.
China-Britain Business Council (CBBC) is the leading organisation helping UK companies grow and develop their business with China. We help companies of all sizes and sectors, whether new entrants or established operations. We offer practical in-market assistance, services, industry initiatives and a membership programme providing access, seminars and networking. Our advantage lies in our 23 local offices throughout both countries, Chinese language understanding, impartial advice, a network of 1,000 members, unique government relations and key contacts in China and the UK. CBBC has worked with China for 60 years and our team has extensive China business experience.
The British Chamber of Commerce in China (BCCC) is a membership organisation for British business focused on boosting UK-China trade and investment. We operate as an independent, not-for-profit organisation with a strong and diverse membership.
The British Chamber of Commerce Guangdong is a dynamic organisation that represents the business interests of our members throughout Guangdong. Our broad spectrum of British, Hong Kong, international and Chinese companies, located in Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Dongguan, Zhuhai and other parts of Guangdong, reflect the huge range of commercial activity taking place in the region.
The BritCham network can support your business throughout its lifecycle from start-up, to development, growth and beyond. No matter where you are based in Guangdong, the type of business you are or whether your objectives are on a local, national or international scale, BritCham GD can help meet your goals.
The British Chamber of Commerce Shanghai, now in its 99th year, has been representing the business interests of its members in the East China region since its establishment in 1915.
The Chamber provides a range of innovative and value-added services for members including a dedicated business development team, business matchmaking and teambuilding activities. Via its China Executives Advisory Group (CEAG), the Chamber facilitates the meeting between visiting company Boards and a select group of executives on the ground to share their view of the challenges and opportunities of doing business in China.
In addition, the Chamber actively engages members in CSR activities and in 2013 raised close to RMB 300,000 for its supported charities.
Today the Chamber comprises over 300 British and international companies with a British interest, ranging from the largest multinationals to start-ups operating in East China.
Export Britain( http://www.exportbritain.org.uk ), the online export support resource from the British Chambers of Commerce, showcases opportunities in key international markets, and links UK exporters to support available from British Chambers and business groups both in the UK and around the world.
Export Britain helps businesses to make new connections overseas by drawing on the power of the global network of British Chambers and business groups. The site also helps growing and prospective exporters navigate through the export journey by signposting the support available every step of the way, as well as providing information on export training and documentation, events and trade missions, and inspiring case studies featuring successful UK exporters.
The British Chambers of Commerce (BCC) sits at the heart of a powerful network of 52 Accredited Chambers of Commerce across the UK, representing thousands of businesses of all sizes and within all sectors. For more information visit: www.britishchambers.org.uk
British Council is the UK’s international organisation for educational opportunities and cultural relations. We create international opportunities for the people of the UK and other countries, and build trust between them worldwide. We have almost 80 years of experience and operate in over 100 countries. Every year we work with thousands of professionals and policy makers and millions of young people through our English, arts, education and society programmes.
The Confederation of British Industry (CBI) is the UK’s premier business lobbying organisation, providing a voice for employers at a national and international level, and has more than 240,000 members. In 2006, the CBI’s Beijing office was established to advance the interests of our members in China.
UK Trade and Investment (UKTI) is a government organisation that helps UK-based companies succeed in an increasingly global economy. UKTI also helps overseas companies bring high quality investment to the UK’s dynamic economy, providing support and advice to investors at all stages of their business. UKTI offers expertise and contacts through its extensive network of specialists in the UK and in British Embassies and other diplomatic offices in more than 110 markets around the world.
The 48 Group Club is an independent business network committed to promoting positive links with China. The Club’s history goes back to the early 1950s, when the founding “Icebreakers” were the first Westerners to break through the worldwide trade embargo on China and establish mutually beneficial trade relations with the newly formed People’s Republic of China. The Club now has over 600 British and Chinese members, both corporate and individual. Member companies and institutions vary in size from the multinational to the micro and come from all sectors: industry, commerce, government, education, sports, culture and the law, and others are high-level politicians, diplomats, academics and individuals and groups focusing on cultural relations.
The largest luxury membership organisation of its kind in Europe, Walpole British Luxury has a far-reaching mission to inspire, develop, protect and promote the industry. It does this through the creation of a community for the exchange of best practice ideas; by developing industry insights, networking opportunities and thought leadership; by lobbying the UK and European governments; and through mentoring programmes to nurture new talent and safeguard the future of the sector.
As the ‘voice of British luxury’ Walpole’s membership is comprised of over 130 of Britain’s most prestigious luxury brands and cultural institutions, supported by more than 20 corporate partners and sponsors, all of whom share a deep-rooted commitment to combining the best aspects of innovation, design, quality, tradition and service. Walpole galvanises the joint strength of this membership by harnessing and sharing its collective knowledge, experience and resources â?? all of which is key in furthering the UK’s international reputation and reach.
