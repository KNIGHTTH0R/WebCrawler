Prime Minister's Questions: David Cameron v Ed Miliband

Key Points

  • Labour leader Ed Miliband welcomed the recent fall in unemployment but raised concerns about Pfizer's proposed takeover of AstraZeneca
  • PM David Cameron maintained he was getting "stuck in" to protect British interests, including high-tech jobs, in the deal
  • Mr Cameron also announced that the UK has offered further assistance to the Nigerian army in the search for the missing schoolgirls
  • Nick Robinson and MPs review PMQs Watch
  • Labour questions Pfizer bid job losses Watch
  • In full: Prime Minister's Questions Watch
  • PM wants Barlow's money back for good Watch
  • Leaders clash on Pfizer AstraZeneca bid Watch

It weas too high, he is told. It is too soon to draw conclusions, he replies. "We are still predicting a very big long-term rise in immigration from Romania and Bulgaria... every sign is that it is going to go up and up, and that is a betrayal of the British people."

  68.  
    11:43:

    On the BBC's Daily Politics programme, UKIP director of communications and MEP candidate Patrick O'Flynn says it came "as a bit of a surprise" that a prominent member of the party's youth wing resigned accusing UKIP of descending into a "form of racist populism". Mr O'Flynn said: "Last week she was singing the praises of UKIP immigration policies."     11:40:

    11:39:

    As usual, there are any number of political stories that could surface above the din. Immigration figures have been released for the first time since visa restrictions were lifted on Bulgaria and Romania (there was no sign of an influx). Unemployment has reportedly hit a five-year low of 2.2 million in the three months to March. And MPs have been putting Pfizer bosses on the spot over their proposed takeover of British firm AstraZeneca.
